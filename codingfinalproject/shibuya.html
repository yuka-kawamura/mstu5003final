Shibuya

1.Take a photo at Hachiko Statue

Hachiko is a famous dog in Japan proved his undying loyalty, waiting for his master's return in the same location every single day for 10 years after his masSter's death. Hachiko, was an Akita dog that belonged to a professor at the University of Tokyo. Every day he would meet his master at Shibuya Station until one day in 1925 when the professor never returned. Unbeknownst to Hachiko, his master haSd suffered a fatal brain hemorrhage and would never come home again. But there Hachiko sat, day after day, waiting in vain for the return of his beloved master. The story of the dog that never gave up gained a lot of attention from local and national news, inspiring many people to visit Hachiko at Shibuya Station to offer treats. The story eventually spread throughout the world and a Hollywood film starring Richard Gere was even made based on Hachiko's story.

2.Walk across the Scramble Crossing

It would be a shame to come to Tokyo and not take a walk across the famous intersection outside Shibuya Station. On sunny afternoons or clear evenings, the surrounding area is packed with shoppers, students, young couples and commuters. When the lights turn red at this busy junction, they all turn red at the same time in every direction. Traffic stops completely and pedestrians surge into the intersection from all sides, like marbles spilling out of a box. You can observe this moment of organized chaos from the second-story window of the Starbucks in the Tsutaya building on the crossing's north side.

3.Shopping at 109

After experiencing the "scramble," follow the trendy teens into Shibuya 109, a big shiny mall with more than 100 boutiques, for a look at the latest in disposable fashion.

4.Walk up to Dougenzaka

Dogenzaka is a famous street in Shibuya lined with restaurants, izakaya, karaoke and shops. The street is a reasonably good way to walk if you're looking for food and nightlife. It leads to an area known as Love Hotel Hill that has several large nightclubs and concert venues.
